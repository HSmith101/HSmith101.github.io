    Official Course Description and Goals

    Official Course Description: Differential and integral calculus of functions of several variables: three di- mensional analytic geometry, vector calculus, partial derivatives, multiple integrals, line integrals, applications, and historical perspectives. Includes a computer laboratory experience. Prerequisite: MATH 162 with a grade of C (2.0) or better.

    Goals: The overarching goal for the semester is to gain a working knowledge of and fluency with multivariate differential and integral calculus. The topics include three dimensional analytic geometry, vector calculus, partial deriva- tives, multiple integrals, line integrals, and their applications. We will work to understand not only computational aspects and definitions, but the parts, pieces, ideas, maps, and pictures that help us solve real world problems with calculus and build on these concepts in future courses.

    One practical goal that will underpin the course is deepening our familiarity with and maturity in mathematics. This includes developing the ability to communicate mathematics and reason mathematically. We will spend our time reading, understanding, responding to, and writing mathematics. A goal of this course is to open and develop this kind of mathematical dialogue with your professor and peers. To this end, we will create a welcoming and supportive classroom community that gives us space to make mistakes and learn.

    Homework

    WebAssign Homework: WebAssign homework assignments will be assigned via WebAssign and due by the posted date and time. See the Course Schedule. The WebAssign software automatically evaluates each response and computes a score for this assignment. Please let me know if you discover an error in the grading. Screenshots are very helpful in this situation. Further, though WebAssign only evaluates the final answer, it is a good idea to write your reasoning down and keep these notes as a reference. Written homework/worksheets and exams will be graded on the entire writeup of the solution, not just the final answer. For more details on WebAssign see https://www.webassign.net/manual/student_guide/introduction.htm.

    In addition to WebAssign, we will have worksheets and written homework. The latter portion of most class meetings will be devoted to working on these assignments in groups. Written homeworks and worksheets will be posted on Canvas. These are due on Gradescope. Due dates are posted in the Course Schedule. Solutions must be clear, complete, and fully justified to receive full credit. Solutions, including equations, should be written in complete sentences. I encourage you to read your solutions aloud. Your peers should be able to read your solutions and clearly understand your reasoning. I encourage you to model your solutions on what is presented in the lecture and in the textbook. Please work with other students and use all resources available to solve the problems. However, your final writeup should be your own work, and all external resources used should be cited.

    It is natural and expected to struggle on homework. You are encouraged to work together and come to office hours for help with homework. Homework is a place to learn how to do things! You may use whatever resources you wish on the homework as long as you cite your sources and submit your own work. Copying and pasting the output of generative AI such as ChatGTP is academically dishonest. You can and should use generative AI resources such as ChatGTP, but you should interpret and personalize your answers, ensuring that they are your own.

    Student Learning Outcomes

    Students will develop a computational and conceptual fluency in the following material:

  • Vectors and three-dimensional coordinate systems including the dot product, cross product, and equations of lines and planes.
  • Vector-valued functions and their derivatives and integrals including an understanding of the connections to position, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Partial derivatives and their applications including tangent planes, linear approximations, directional derivates, the gradient, and specific applications to max/min problems.
  • Multiple integrals including double integrals in polar coordinates, triple integrals in cylindrical and spherical coordinates, and changing variables in multiple integrals.
  • Vector Calculus including the fundamental theorem for line integrals, Green's theorem, and basic perspectives on Stokes' theorem. Underpinning this fluency is developing and deepening connections between these concepts and other mathematical concepts that students have encountered both formally and informally.

    BS in Mathematics Student Learning Outcomes:
    Students who graduate with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics will be able to:
    1. Apply the core concepts of algebra and analysis.
    2. Explain mathematical ideas, written and verbally, in a clear and organized way.
    3. Develop and write mathematical proofs.
    4. Apply mathematical algorithms and use appropriate technology for the solution of mathematical problems and analysis of real world models.
    5. Recognize the interdependency of different areas of mathematics, as well as connections between mathematics and other disciplines.

    Academic Integrity

    Students will be expected to adhere to the guideline of academic honesty and integrity, as outlined in the CSUSM Standards for Student Conduct. All assignments must be original work, clear and error-free. All ideas/material that are borrowed from other sources must have appropriate references to the original sources. Any quoted material should give credit to the source and be punctuated accordingly.

    Students are responsible for honest completion and representation of their work. Unless otherwise noted, all completed assignments are to consist solely of your own individual work and ideas without input from any external resources or persons. There will be zero tolerance for infractions. If you believe there has been an infraction by someone in the class, please bring it to the my attention. I reserve the right to discipline any student for academic dishonesty, in accordance with the general rules and regulations of the university. Disciplinary action may include the lowering of grades and/or the assignment of a failing grade for an exam, assignment, or the class as a whole. It is recommended that students consult the CSUSM Academic Honesty Policy.

    As a four unit course, the award of credit in this course is based on learning activities outside of class meetings having been designed to require a minimum of eight hours of effort each week working for most students.
